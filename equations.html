Maxwell's Equations (in free space)

  • Gauss's Law for Electricity:
    $$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}$$
  • Gauss's Law for Magnetism:
    $$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$$
  • Faraday's Law of Induction:
    $$\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}$$
  • Ampère's Law (with Maxwell's addition):
    $$\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \mathbf{J} + \mu_0 \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}$$

Wave Equation

Describes propagation of electromagnetic waves:

$$\nabla^2 \mathbf{E} - \mu_0 \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2} = 0$$

Optics Concepts & Equations

  • Snell's Law:
    $$n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2$$
  • Refractive Index:
    $$n = \frac{c}{v}$$ (where $c$ is speed of light in vacuum, $v$ in material)
  • Critical Angle:
    $$\theta_c = \arcsin\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right)$$ (for $n_1 > n_2$)
  • Fresnel Equations:
    Describe reflection and transmission at interfaces.
  • Lens Equation:
    $$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}$$
  • Diffraction Grating:
    $$d \sin \theta = m \lambda$$

Short Explanations

  • Maxwell's Equations: Fundamental laws describing how electric and magnetic fields interact and propagate.
  • Wave Equation: Derived from Maxwell's equations, describes how light travels as a wave.
  • Snell's Law: Governs refraction of light at material boundaries.
  • Refractive Index: Ratio of speed of light in vacuum to that in material.
  • Critical Angle: Angle above which total internal reflection occurs.
  • Lens Equation: Relates object and image distances to focal length.
  • Diffraction Grating: Describes how light is dispersed by a grating.
